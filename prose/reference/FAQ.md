# TongueToQuill — Frequently Asked Questions

---

## General

### What is TongueToQuill?

TongueToQuill is a free, browser-based editor for U.S. Air Force and Space Force personnel. You write your document content in plain text, and TongueToQuill automatically formats and exports it as a regulation-compliant PDF — no fighting with Word margins or template files required. Formatting follows **AFH 33-337 "The Tongue and Quill"** standards automatically.

---

### Is TongueToQuill free?

Yes — and there's no software to install. Sign in at [tonguetoquill.com](https://tonguetoquill.com) with a Google or GitHub account and you're ready to go.

---

### What kinds of documents can TongueToQuill produce?

- **Letters of Counseling (LOC)**
- **Letters of Reprimand (LOR)**
- **Rebuttals**
- **Official Memorandums** (USAF and USSF variants)
- **Memoranda for Record (MFR)**

Both Air Force and Space Force formatting variants are supported, with automatic letterhead, hierarchical paragraph numbering, indorsement chains, attachments, CC, and distribution lists.

---

### Is TongueToQuill officially endorsed by the Air Force or Space Force?

TongueToQuill at tonguetoquill.com is an independent, community-built tool. However, **[Airmark](https://airmark.omni.af.mil)** — the IL5-accredited deployment of the same platform — is hosted by the **55th Wing** on Air Force infrastructure. If your unit requires an officially sanctioned option, Airmark is the appropriate choice.

---

### Should I type classified or sensitive information into TongueToQuill?

**No.** TongueToQuill at tonguetoquill.com is a commercial web application and is not accredited or authorized for classified information, Controlled Unclassified Information (CUI), or Privacy Act-protected data. Use it for unclassified, non-sensitive drafts only.

If you need to produce memos with CUI or other sensitive content, use the Air Force's IL5-accredited deployment at **[airmark.omni.af.mil](https://airmark.omni.af.mil)**, which runs on the same platform in an approved environment.

---

## Writing and Editing

### Do I need to know Markdown?

No. The default **Rich Text** mode works like a normal document editor — fill in form fields for the header information (receiver, subject, date, etc.) and type your memo body using a formatting toolbar. No Markdown knowledge required.

If you're comfortable with Markdown, you can switch to **Advanced** mode to write the raw source directly. Both modes produce the same output.

---

### Does TongueToQuill use AI?

No — TongueToQuill is a **formatter**, not a writing assistant. You supply the words; it handles the layout, margins, paragraph numbering, and AFH 33-337 compliance. If you want to draft content with an AI tool first and then paste it in to format, that works fine — just apply the same judgment you would with any external service.

---

### Will the output actually pass formatting review?

TongueToQuill is built specifically against the AFH 33-337 standard — margins, paragraph numbering, letterhead, indorsements, and all. In practice, output is consistently cleaner than hand-formatted Word documents. That said, every unit has its own quirks, and TongueToQuill cannot account for local guidance that goes beyond the standard.

---

## Privacy and Data

### Does TongueToQuill store my documents?

When you are signed in, documents are saved to a secure database associated with your account. Guest (unsigned-in) documents are stored temporarily and deleted automatically. No document content is shared with third parties.

## Fonts

### Why does my exported PDF look slightly different from a memo produced in Microsoft Word?

The official USAF memo format calls for **Copperplate Gothic** in the letterhead and **Times New Roman** in the body. Both are commercial, proprietary fonts that cannot legally be distributed as part of free software.

TongueToQuill uses two freely distributable look-alike fonts as replacements:

| Official font | What TongueToQuill uses |
|---|---|
| Copperplate Gothic | **CopperplateCC-Heavy** — an open-source Copperplate clone |
| Times New Roman | **NimbusRomNo9L** — an open-source Times Roman clone |

These substitutes are designed to match the spacing and proportions of the originals, so layout and line breaks will be correct. The visual difference from a Word-produced memo is minimal and should not affect document acceptance.
